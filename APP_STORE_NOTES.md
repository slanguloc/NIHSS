## App description (suggested opening)

**Zysquy — NIH Stroke Scale for non-English speaking patients**

**For education and training only. Not for clinical use.**

Zysquy helps learners and educators practice the NIH Stroke Scale (NIHSS) with support for non-English speaking patients (e.g. Spanish). It provides step-by-step guidance and ready phrases for use in training and simulation—not for use in patient care or clinical decision-making.

---

## Notes for App Review (paste in “Notes” for the reviewer)

This app is for **education and training only**. It is **not intended for clinical use** and must not be used to make or support clinical decisions, diagnoses, or treatment. It is not a medical device. It is designed for learning and practicing the NIH Stroke Scale and for educational support when working with non-English speaking patients in a training context only. The in-app disclaimer “For education only. Not for clinical use.” is shown on the home screen.

## Privacy policy

**The app does not collect any data.** All information is stored only on the user’s device and is encrypted for patient information safety. Use the repo file **PRIVACY_POLICY.md** as your privacy policy (host it at a URL and add that URL in App Store Connect → App Privacy / App Information).

Summary for the listing:
- **No data collection** — We do not collect, transmit, or share any user data.
- **Local only** — All data (e.g. language preference, assessment history) stays on the device.
- **Encrypted** — Stored data is encrypted on-device for patient information safety.
